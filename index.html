Freelance Pricing & Rate Guide

Understand the mathematics of profitable freelancing. Learn how to calculate quotes, set income targets, and optimize your rates.

How Much Should I Charge as a Freelancer?

Determining your freelance hourly rate is one of the most critical decisions you make for your business. Many freelancers make the mistake of setting rates based solely on what they want to earn per hour, without accounting for self-employment taxes, business overhead, and unpaid work. To find your ideal rate, start with your annual income target, add your expected business expenses and tax liabilities, and divide the total by your billable hours. Using a dedicated freelancer hourly rate calculator helps automate this analysis and ensures you remain profitable.

Project Pricing vs. Hourly Rate Calculator

Fixed-fee project pricing can yield high margins but carries the risk of scope creep—where client revisions and extra calls consume your hours without increasing your pay. Loomrate acts as a project quote calculator, helping you translate any fixed project fee into an equivalent hourly rate. By comparing your gross quoted rate to your true net take-home rate, you can make informed decisions before signing contracts and ensure your pricing strategies match realistic income targets.

Accounting for Self-Employment Taxes & Overhead

Unlike traditional employees, freelancers are responsible for 100% of their operational expenses and tax allocations. These include platform commissions (e.g., Upwork or Fiverr fees), software subscriptions, hardware depreciation, and tax reserves. Loomrate's freelance pricing calculator automatically subtracts these overhead costs from your gross billing fee, exposing what you actually keep. Understanding your net income per project is key to scaling your consulting business.
